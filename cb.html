Chicken Biriyani


Ingredients:

  1. Basmati Rice
  2. Chicken
  3. Onion
  4. Whole Spices - cinnamon, cloves, fennel seeds, cumin seeds, bay leaf, cardamom
  5. Fresh Herbs - lots of chopped coriander leaves and mint leaves
  6. Spice powders - a combination of chilli powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder
  7. 1 stick Cinnamon
  8. 2 tsp Fennel Seeds
  9. 1 tsp Cumin Seeds
  10. 2 Bay Leaf
  11. 5 Green Cardamom
  12. 1 Black Cardamom
  13. 4 Cloves
  14. 2 Mace
  15. 4 Kalpasi
  16. 3 tbsp Coriander Seeds
  17. 4 Dry Red Chilli
  18. 1 Star Anise
  19. 1 tsp Whole Black Pepper
  20. Curd
  21. Ghee or Oil

Instructions:

  1. Take 500 grams of cleaned chicken in a bowl. I am using skinless and bone-in chicken. Make sure you cut the chicken into medium pieces which cooks fast and stays moist. To the cleaned chicken, add in curd, salt, chilli powder, garam masala powder and ginger garlic paste. Mix well and set aside to marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. While the chicken is marinating. Wash 2 cups of basmati rice multiple times till water runs clear. Cover with fresh cold water and let it soak for 30 minutes.
  3. Take fennel seeds, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, mace, star anise in a blender and powder till coarse. This is your fresh masala for biryani.
  4. Heat coconut oil and ghee in a kadai. Add in sliced onions and slit green chillies. Saute them till light golden in colour.
  5. Add in freshly ground ginger and garlic paste. Saute the ginger and garlic till raw smell leaves. It will take around 2 minutes.
  6. Add in chilli powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder and fresh garam masala powder.
  7. Saute the spice powders with onion for few seconds.
  8. once masala is cooked, add in the marinated chicken.
  9. Saute the marinated chicken in the masala for at least 6 to 8 minutes. The chicken will be half cooked at this point.
  10. Take this chicken masala in a pressure cooker.
  11. In the same pan in which you made the chicken masala. Add a tbsp of ghee.
  12. Add in the 2 cups of soaked strained rice. Roast the rice for few minutes till it turns toasty and light golden in colour.
  13. Add in the roasted rice over the chicken masala which is kept in the pressure cooker.
  14. Add in chopped coriander and mint leaves.
  15. Pour in 1 cup of coconut milk. This is optional. You can use water instead of coconut milk.
  16. Pour in 2 cups of water.
  17. Add salt to taste.
  18. Bring this to a full boil. Once it starts boiling give it one good stir. cover the lid of the pressure cooker.
  19. Pressure cook for 1 whistle, reduce the flame to lowest possible and cook for 5 minutes. Switch off the flame and let the pressure release by itself.
  20. Open the cooker and mix gently using a spatula or fork.
  21. Serve.
