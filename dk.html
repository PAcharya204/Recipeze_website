Dhokla


Ingredients:

  1. 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  2. 2 tbsp semolina (rava/suji) – optional for extra softness
  3. 1 tsp ginger-green chili paste
  4. 1 tbsp lemon juice
  5. 1 tbsp sugar
  6. 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  7. Salt to taste
  8. 1 cup water
  9. 1 tsp fruit salt (Eno) or 1/2 tsp baking soda
  10. 2 tbsp oil
  11. 1 tsp mustard seeds
  12. 1 tsp sesame seeds
  13. 2 green chilies, slit
  14. 8–10 curry leaves
  15. 1/4 cup water
  16. 1 tsp sugar
  17. Freshly grated coconut (optional)
  18. Chopped coriander leaves

Instructions:

  1. Wash the raw rice thoroughly until the water runs clear.Soak the rice in enough water for 4–6 hours or overnight.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine gram flour, semolina (if using), turmeric powder, salt, sugar, and ginger-green chili paste.
  3. Add water gradually and whisk to make a smooth, lump-free batter.
  4. Add lemon juice and mix well. Keep the batter slightly thick but flowing.
  5. Just before steaming, add Eno or baking soda. Mix gently but quickly. The batter will become airy and light.
  6. Grease a steaming plate or tray with oil.
  7. Pour the batter into the greased plate and spread it evenly.
  8. Heat water in a steamer or large pot and place a stand in the center. Once the water starts boiling, place the plate with the batter on the stand.
  9. Cover with a lid and steam for 15–20 minutes on medium heat. Check with a toothpick – it should come out clean when inserted into the dhokla.
  10. Once steamed, let it cool slightly and cut into squares or diamonds.
  11. Heat oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  12. Add sesame seeds, green chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté for a few seconds.
  13. Add 1/4 cup water and sugar, and stir until the sugar dissolves. Turn off the heat.
  14. Pour the prepared tempering evenly over the dhokla pieces, ensuring it seeps in.
  15. Garnish with grated coconut and chopped coriander leaves.
